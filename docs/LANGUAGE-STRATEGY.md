# Portfolio Language Strategy

## Purpose

All public, agency-facing portfolio materials should demonstrate that language differences do not prevent clear, reliable collaboration.

## Primary languages

- English — primary business and technical language
- Japanese — localized presentation for Japanese studios and agencies

## Required bilingual materials

The following materials should be available in both English and Japanese:

- Portfolio website navigation and core pages
- Project summaries and case studies
- Services and collaboration model
- Contact and availability information
- Partner Kit
- Agency outreach templates for Japanese companies

## Positioning

The portfolio must not claim native or professional Japanese fluency unless that becomes accurate.

Recommended wording:

> English is the primary working language. Japanese-language project materials, documentation, and written communication can be prepared with translation support and careful review.

Japanese version:

> 主な業務言語は英語です。日本語でのプロジェクト資料、ドキュメント、書面でのコミュニケーションについても、翻訳支援と慎重な確認を行いながら対応可能です。

## Communication model

For Japanese agency cooperation, the workflow should support:

1. Written briefs and task specifications
2. Bilingual summaries of requirements
3. Visual references, Figma files, tickets, and acceptance criteria
4. Written progress updates
5. Documented decisions and change requests
6. Final delivery notes in English and Japanese when required

## Quality rules

- English remains the canonical source for technical accuracy.
- Japanese content must be reviewed before publication.
- Machine translation must never be presented as native fluency.
- Technical terms should remain consistent across both versions.
- Short, precise business language is preferred over decorative copy.

## Goal

The bilingual structure should communicate three things:

- readiness to work with international teams,
- respect for Japanese clients and studios,
- a practical process that reduces language risk.
